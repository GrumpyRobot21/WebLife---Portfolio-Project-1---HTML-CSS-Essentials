About us.

While it is not always an easy thing to consider, reviewing and compiling your digital life to plan ahead for when you’re no longer around to access it is crucial to guaranteeing your private information can be conscientiously taken care of. This is a subject that online platforms are increasingly aware of and many now allow you to issue instructions for what should happen in the event of your death. However, depending on the particular platform, both preserving and/or deleting your account are possible, but it does require forethought, and they all have very differing levels of complexity in their approaches.

Until recently, our treasured memories and important documents have been traditionally kept as physical objects. e.g. - photo albums, scrapbooks, letters, diaries, contracts, official documentation. Usually all of these physical items would be available to loved ones once we were no longer here. However, since the rise of the internet, especially cloud based storage, everything is now in a digital format. From audio and video files to our huge photo collections, emails, documents, contracts and our social media profiles – none of these are easily accessible by third parties following ones death without legacy planning in advance.

WebLife approaches this requirement with your specific wishes in mind. It is managed by your unique legacy administrator. This is the only person you will deal with at WebLife ensuring a close working relationship and a single point of contact without having to discuss your private and sensitive information with multiple entities. Following our clients express wishes, WebLife's sole responsibility is to erase and/or manage their online presence and sensitive information over social media, email, online retail, online gaming, banking, in preparation for when they are no longer able to. WebLife also makes sure that our clients express wishes are respected in accordance with their specific, lawful and legally binding final requests.
